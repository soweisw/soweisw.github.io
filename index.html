         Hello! I'm Sophie, and welcome to my website! I'm a Summa Cum Laude computer science graduate from the University of Michigan who loves building games, tools, and systems that feel good to interact with for both users and programmers.
         I've been programming since elementary school, and I've taken a particular liking to game development. I co-created Night of the Living Ducks, a cooperative indie game that placed third out of eighteen teams at a university showcase, and later helped to reimagine and publish H4CT0PL45M on Steam, transforming a simple prototype into a fully themed experience about a ghost hacker navigating a digital world. From gameplay systems and UI to pixel art, 3D models, music, and level design, I enjoy shaping projects end-to-end and making sure every piece works cohesively.
         Beyond games, I've built natural language logic processors, performed cybersecurity forensics, and designed large-scale archival database systems — experiences that sharpened my attention to structure, clarity, and clean code. Whether I'm leading a team, refining mechanics, or architecting backend systems, I care deeply about doing meaningful work and doing it well.
